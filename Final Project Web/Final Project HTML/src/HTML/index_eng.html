The app towards a greener lifestyle.

Problem

How can we shift todays focus from the problem itself and how bad things have become, to what we can do to create a positive change in the development?

Our vision

Our vision is to create a platform where children and adults can come together and learn how everyone can reduce their own climate footprint – and thus create a community where we all contribute. The greatest impact is made by us, together.

Solution

A product which is going to create a solid base for a better understanding of how and why the climate is being affected by the choices that we make.

Problem


“I would like to have a debate about the climate, without us having to talk about a doomsday”.

The above was stated by the Danish child’s psychologist Margrethe Brun Hansen (1948 – to this day) earlier this year, and her opinion is that “we are in the process of creating a whole generation of children with eco anxiety”. The exposure of the climate problems and how bad the challenges regarding this have become, have had a steep climb the last couple of years. We can’t open a paper or search the web without hearing and reading about numbers from climate scientists and environmental organizations regarding how much the sea level will rise over the next few years, how many animal species are endangered, or how much the ocean is polluted by garbage and plastic. It is easy to feel dejected, scared and depressed by the flow of facts that are presented by the media. The hope, solutions and determination are struggling to find its place and visibility amongst the “destructive talk”.

“Eco anxiety” has become an expression these days, and it is defined by the increasing worries and feeling of helplessness about the negative effect we have had on the climate so far. According to NRK, there is a study executed in 2019 by the Center for International Climate and Environmental Research Oslo (Cicero), showing that “nearly 60 percent of people under the age of 30 is pretty much or very much concerned about the climate changes” (see picture further down). Another survey called “Washington Post-Kaiser Family Foundation Climate Change Survey” that was performed amongst children between the age of 13 and 17, show us that 57% of the ones asked answered “yes” to the question “does the issue of climate change make you feel afraid?” (see picture further down). Both of these surveys shows us that the focus is too strong on the problems themselves and (the definition of) how bad it has become – and not nearly enough on what we actually can do to make a change.

The climate changes have now become a crucial and defining problem of our time – and we must give it the attention it deserves. The consequences of this is becoming more evident, and the price have to pay is growing. How can we help changing the focus, and inspire people to take part in the fight for a better environment and climate? In which way can we increase the interest and motivation when it comes to making choices in our everyday life that can change everyone’s climate footprint – and together lower the total emission of greenhouse gases?

Our solution


In the future, we need to focus on solutions, commitments and most of all – action. We all have to start somewhere, and it is important to emphasize a phrase in our vision – the fact that it is impossible for anyone to do everything, but everyone can do something. This is a fundamental and an important message, and our opinion is that this is one of the main reasons that people feel that being climate and environmentally friendly is an almost impossible task. In the light of all the challenges we are facing, our own choices and actions seem small and insignificant.

To be able to work towards reducing the total greenhouse gas emission that exists on this planet, we have to start by putting our own emissions in the spotlight. What is your climate footprint? What can you do to diminish this? Which actions do you need to focus on? The first step is to understand how and why your choices are affecting the environment, so that we can form our actions from actual knowledge and facts.

Our app, ecoCentric, is creating a platform that will contribute to inspire and motivate the users, to start cooperating for a better climate and environment. The product will also provide the possibility for a better understanding of how and why the climate is being affected by our choices, and we think that this comprehension can contribute to reduce the destructive relationship that we have to the climate changes and the evolvement in the future. We are all a part of the problem – but we are all a part of the solution as well.


We believe that getting an overview of the actions you do to improve the quality of our environment and climate, will give you an affirmation of the fact that your actions are of great significance. At the same time, it is important to feel that you are not alone in all of this, and that is why we wish to present ecoCentric as a social platform.

If you are not able to click through the app-images, downsize your browser and get access to a slideshow.

Home

Explore your homepage where you can get a quick overview of your total carbon print, and the possibility to explore the different categories you have emissions in. Check off your personal list when you have reached your defined goals to reduce your climate footprint. Get daily tips and tricks to increase your consciousness and knowledge about what creates a climate footprint.

Profile

Dive deeper into your biggest consumptions, discover what have the biggest emissions and get a graphic overview of how your footprint has evolved over time. Add friends and stay updated and connected with them as you give and get information about how you make climate-friendly choices in your everyday life.

Monthly Challenges

Collaborate with the rest of the users in ecoCentric and work on a new challenge every month. Get an overview of how much your common carbon print have been reduced and encourage each other to pull through the challenges together.

Recycle

Get access to a map of recycling points near you, where you can hand over hazardous waste, clothes, recycle bottles etc. You will also be able to scan the barcode of a product if you are unsure about where it should be sorted and get information about where to throw and/or deliver it.
